# 📄 Parts of a Research Paper (Professional Structure)

## 1. Title Page
- Paper title  
- Author’s name  
- Institution (college/university)  
- Course details  
- Date  

---

## 2. Abstract
- A brief summary (150–250 words)  

**Includes:**
- Research problem  
- Method  
- Key findings  
- Conclusion  

---

## 3. Introduction
- Background of the study  
- Research problem  
- Research objectives  
- Research questions  
- Significance of the study  

> 👉 This section sets the context for your research.

---

## 4. Literature Review
- Review of previous studies  
- Theories related to your topic  
- Identifies research gaps  

> 👉 Shows what others have done and where your research fits.

---

## 5. Theoretical Framework *(Optional but important in many fields)*
- The theory you are using  
- Explanation of concepts  

> 👉 Example: Postcolonial theory, media theory, etc.

---

## 6. Methodology (Research Method)
- Type of research (qualitative/quantitative)  
- Research design  
- Data collection methods  
- Sample selection  
- Tools used  

> 👉 Explains how you conducted your research.

---

## 7. Data Analysis / Results
- Presentation of findings  
- Tables, charts, or textual analysis  

> 👉 No opinions here—just results.

---

## 8. Discussion
- Interpretation of results  
- Link findings with theory and literature  

> 👉 Explains what the results mean.

---

## 9. Conclusion
- Summary of findings  
- Final answer to research question  
- Implications  
- Suggestions for future research  

---

## 10. References / Bibliography
- List of all sources used  
- Proper citation style (APA, MLA, etc.)  

---

## 11. Appendices *(Optional)*
- Questionnaires  
- Raw data  
- Charts
