# Kazi Nazrul Islam and His Revolutionary Songs

## 1. Introduction

Kazi Nazrul Islam was a Bengali poet, musician, and revolutionary who used his writings and songs to oppose British colonial rule in India. His musical works, known as *Nazrul Geeti*, played a powerful role in inspiring resistance and unity among people.

---

## 2. Number of Patriotic / Anti-British Songs

- Nazrul composed **over 4,000 songs** in total.
- Among them, **hundreds were patriotic and revolutionary** in nature.
- Many of these songs were **banned by the British government**.
- There is no exact number of “jatiya sangeet” (national songs), but **many became popular protest songs**.

---

## 3. Famous Anti-British / Revolutionary Songs

### 3.1 Karar Oi Louho Kopat

- **Meaning:** “Those iron prison gates”
- **Theme:** Oppression, prison, revolution
- **Purpose:** Inspire prisoners and freedom fighters
- **Message:** Break the chains of slavery

**Impact:**
- Widely sung by political prisoners
- Became a symbol of resistance inside British jails

---

### 3.2 Bidrohi (The Rebel)

- **Meaning:** A declaration of rebellion
- **Theme:** Strength, defiance, revolution
- **Purpose:** Awaken courage among people
- **Message:** Resistance against injustice and oppression

**Impact:**
- Earned Nazrul the title **“Bidrohi Kobi” (Rebel Poet)**
- Inspired youth to oppose colonial rule

---

### 3.3 Chal Chal Chal

- **Meaning:** “March forward”
- **Theme:** Unity, movement, progress
- **Purpose:** Motivate people to take action
- **Message:** Keep moving forward toward freedom

**Impact:**
- Later became a national marching song in Bangladesh
- Still used in military and patriotic events

---

### 3.4 Durgam Giri Kantar Moru

- **Meaning:** Crossing difficult mountains and deserts
- **Theme:** Struggle, perseverance
- **Purpose:** Encourage people to face hardships
- **Message:** Freedom requires sacrifice and determination

**Impact:**
- Inspired freedom fighters during protests

---

### 3.5 Jago Anashan Bondi

- **Meaning:** “Wake up, fasting prisoners”
- **Theme:** Protest, hunger strike
- **Purpose:** Support political prisoners
- **Message:** Rise and resist injustice

**Impact:**
- Closely associated with prison protests and political resistance

---

## 4. Purpose of His Songs

Nazrul’s revolutionary songs served several important purposes:

### 4.1 To Awaken People
- Help people understand the reality of colonial oppression

### 4.2 To Inspire Rebellion
- Encourage active resistance against British rule

### 4.3 To Promote Unity
- Unite Hindus and Muslims against a common enemy

### 4.4 To Give Courage
- Strengthen the morale of freedom fighters and prisoners

---

## 5. Results and Impact

### 5.1 British Government Reaction
- His writings and songs were **banned**
- His journal *Dhumketu* was shut down
- He was **imprisoned**

---

### 5.2 Influence on Society
- Songs were widely used in:
  - Protests
  - Political movements
  - Student activism
  - Prisons

---

### 5.3 Cultural Legacy
- Became a lasting symbol of resistance
- His songs are still:
  - Studied
  - Performed
  - Celebrated

---

### 5.4 Long-term Impact
- Strengthened anti-colonial awareness
- Contributed to freedom movements in India and Bangladesh

---

## 6. Conclusion

Kazi Nazrul Islam used music and poetry as powerful tools against British colonial rule. His songs were not just artistic expressions but instruments of resistance that inspired courage, unity, and the desire for freedom. His legacy continues to influence generations even today.
