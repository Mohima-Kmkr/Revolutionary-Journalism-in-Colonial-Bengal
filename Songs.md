# Kazi Nazrul Islam and His Revolutionary Songs (Detailed Explanation)

## 1. Introduction

Kazi Nazrul Islam was not only a poet and musician but also a revolutionary thinker who actively opposed British colonial rule in India. Living during a time when freedom movements were rising, Nazrul used **songs, poems, and journalism** as tools of resistance.

His music, known as *Nazrul Geeti*, combined:
- Strong political messages  
- Emotional appeal  
- Cultural unity  

Unlike many writers of his time, Nazrul directly **challenged authority**, which made his work powerful but also dangerous under colonial rule.

---

## 2. Number of Patriotic / Anti-British Songs

- Nazrul composed **more than 4,000 songs** in his lifetime.
- Out of these, a **large portion (hundreds)** were:
  - Patriotic  
  - Anti-colonial  
  - Revolutionary  

### Why there is no exact number:
- His songs were not officially categorized at the time.
- Many songs spread orally among people.
- Some works were **lost or censored by the British government**.

### Important Point:
Nazrul’s patriotic songs were not always labeled as “national songs,” but their **content and impact made them function like national anthems for protest movements**.

---

## 3. Famous Anti-British / Revolutionary Songs (Detailed)

### 3.1 Karar Oi Louho Kopat

- **Meaning:** “Those iron prison gates”
- **Context:** Written when many freedom fighters were imprisoned.

#### Deeper Meaning:
- The “iron gates” symbolize **British oppression and imprisonment**.
- The song encourages prisoners to mentally break free even if physically confined.

#### Purpose:
- Boost morale of jailed revolutionaries  
- Remind them that imprisonment is temporary, but freedom is inevitable  

#### Result:
- Became a **symbol of resistance in jails**
- Strengthened unity among prisoners  
- Spread secretly despite restrictions  

---

### 3.2 Bidrohi (The Rebel)

- **Meaning:** A powerful declaration of rebellion
- Though originally a poem, it was widely recited and performed like a song.

#### Deeper Meaning:
- The speaker represents **universal rebellion** against injustice.
- Uses strong imagery (storm, fire, destruction) to show power.

#### Purpose:
- Inspire fearlessness among people  
- Break psychological submission to colonial power  

#### Result:
- Made Nazrul famous as **“Bidrohi Kobi” (Rebel Poet)**
- Became a motivational piece for youth and activists  

---

### 3.3 Chal Chal Chal

- **Meaning:** “March forward, keep moving”
- A rhythmic and energetic song.

#### Deeper Meaning:
- Movement represents **progress toward freedom**
- Encourages continuous effort despite obstacles

#### Purpose:
- Mobilize masses  
- Encourage discipline and unity  

#### Result:
- Later adopted as a **national marching song in Bangladesh**
- Still used in patriotic ceremonies  

---

### 3.4 Durgam Giri Kantar Moru

- **Meaning:** “Cross difficult mountains and deserts”

#### Deeper Meaning:
- Mountains and deserts symbolize **hardships in the freedom struggle**
- Suggests that struggle is necessary for achieving independence

#### Purpose:
- Prepare people mentally for sacrifice  
- Encourage endurance  

#### Result:
- Motivated activists during difficult phases of resistance  
- Reinforced idea that freedom requires effort  

---

### 3.5 Jago Anashan Bondi

- **Meaning:** “Wake up, fasting prisoners”

#### Deeper Meaning:
- Refers to hunger strikes by political prisoners  
- Encourages awareness and resistance even in suffering  

#### Purpose:
- Support political prisoners  
- Inspire continued protest inside prisons  

#### Result:
- Strengthened prison resistance movements  
- Spread awareness about prisoner struggles  

---

## 4. Purpose of His Songs (Expanded)

Nazrul’s songs were created with clear revolutionary intentions:

### 4.1 To Awaken Political Consciousness
- Many people were unaware of the depth of British exploitation  
- His songs explained oppression in emotional and relatable ways  

---

### 4.2 To Inspire Active Resistance
- Not just awareness, but action  
- Encouraged protests, rebellion, and courage  

---

### 4.3 To Promote Hindu-Muslim Unity
- British used “divide and rule” policy  
- Nazrul emphasized unity across religions  

---

### 4.4 To Empower the Oppressed
- Focused on:
  - Prisoners  
  - Workers  
  - Youth  
- Gave them a voice and identity  

---

## 5. Results and Impact (Expanded)

### 5.1 British Government Reaction

Nazrul’s works were seen as dangerous:

- His writings were **banned or censored**
- His journal *Dhumketu* was **shut down**
- He was **arrested and imprisoned**

This shows how seriously the British took his influence.

---

### 5.2 Influence on Society

His songs became tools of resistance:

- Sung in **protests and rallies**
- Used in **student movements**
- Shared in **secret gatherings**
- Sung inside **prisons**

They acted like:
> “Emotional fuel for the freedom struggle”

---

### 5.3 Cultural and Political Legacy

Nazrul’s impact continued beyond his time:

- Became a **symbol of rebellion and justice**
- His songs are still:
  - Studied in schools  
  - Performed in cultural programs  
  - Used in national events  

---

### 5.4 Long-term Impact

- Helped build **anti-colonial awareness**
- Strengthened independence movements in:
  - India  
  - Bangladesh  

- Influenced future writers and activists  

---

## 6. Conclusion

Kazi Nazrul Islam transformed music into a powerful political weapon. His songs:
- Challenged colonial authority  
- Inspired courage and unity  
- Strengthened the freedom movement  

Even today, his works remind us that **art can play a major role in social and political change**.
