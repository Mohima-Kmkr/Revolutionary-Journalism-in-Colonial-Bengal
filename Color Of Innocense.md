# 🎬 Colours of Innocence  
*(Original title: Sahaj Paather Gappo)*

---

## 1. Basic Filmography & Production Context

- **Director & Screenplay:** Manas Mukul Pal  
- **Producer:** Avijit Saha  
- **Source Text:** *Taal Nabami* by Bibhutibhushan Bandyopadhyay  
- **Release Year:** 2016  
- **Language:** Bengali  
- **Genre:** Social realist drama / coming-of-age  
- **Runtime:** ~81–82 minutes  

### Production Features
- Shot across **three seasons** (post-monsoon, autumn, spring) to capture rural temporality  
- Filmed in **Birbhum (Bolpur)** and **South 24 Parganas**  
- Cast primarily **non-professional child actors**  

> Aligns with **Italian Neorealism** and Bengali realist traditions.

---

## 2. Narrative Structure (Plot Analysis)

The narrative follows **Gopal (10 years old)** and his younger brother **Chhotu**, whose life changes after their father’s accident.

### Core Narrative Arc:
- **Disruption:** Father’s accident → economic collapse  
- **Adaptation:** Gopal takes up informal labour (well-cleaning, fruit selling)  
- **Aspiration:** Desire to attend Janmashtami feast  
- **Disillusionment:** Exclusion from feast → class divide  
- **Emotional Climax:** Collapse of Chhotu’s expectations  

> Linear realist narrative with episodic survival fragments.

### a. Poverty and Childhood
- Child labour as necessity  
- Loss of innocence due to economic hardship  

> Linked to **Marxist theory**: material conditions shape life experience.

---

### b. Class Exclusion & Ritual Power
- Janmashtami feast as a symbol of:
  - Social hierarchy  
  - Cultural exclusion  

> Reflects **Gramscian hegemony**.

---

### c. Rural Realism
- Focus on agrarian Bengal  
- Highlights:
  - Labour economy  
  - Seasonal cycles  
  - Community hierarchy  

> Fits within **ethnographic cinema traditions**.

---

### d. Innocence vs Reality
- Title is ironic  
- Innocence gradually eroded by structural inequality
